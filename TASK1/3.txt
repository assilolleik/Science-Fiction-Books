Natalie frowned. This was the only room in the small house she wasn’t allowed to invade with her rags and buckets and brooms since all the scraps of paper lying about—filled with the professor’s notations—could contain answers to everything. Not wanting to be held accountable for throwing away any priceless solutions to universal questions, she cleaned only as far as the professor’s door, eyes open for unusual junk her vacuum cleaner might eat. She switched off the machine. "You have to get up now, Professor. otherwise you won’t get much sleep tonight. Come on, nap time is over."
